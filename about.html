About

I'm Jonathan Wilde, the photographer/developer behind SpeedBreeze. I spend my time in school developing skills that will hopefully allow me to someday change the world through the creative use of design and software development skills.

To achieve this, I continually work to nurture a multitude of skills in a wide range of fields. Whenever possible, I let these (often very different) skills collide and intersect (e.g. developing software for my stage management work).

Learning

I've spent the last eight years of my life learning all sorts of programming languages, including:

  • Visual Basic (6 & .Net) (2002 - 2006)
  • HTML, XHTML, and XML (2003 - today)
  • CSS (2004 - today)
  • C (2004 - 2007)
  • PHP (2005 - today)
  • JavaScript (2006 - today)
  • Python (2008 - today)
  • Java (2009 - today)

Am I a super-amazing developer yet? No. Far from it. I still have a lot to learn. I need to build more formal computer science experience. I need to experience many more lectures and many more exams. I still have a long way to go.

Recognition

Google Highly Open Participation Contest - Grand Prize Winner, Plone (2007)

During the competition, I wrote extensions for the Plone content management system. I developed 4 themes (which give Plone a unique look-and-feel) and 2 portlets (which allow website designers to place content in the sidebars of their website). I was selected as the grand prize winner for Plone because I developed a large quantity of high-quality work in a matter of days. The Plone team liked extensive web browser testing to ensure that my work was robust.
